Research Statement

My primary research interest lies in understanding the internal decision-making processes of deep learning models. I focus on identifying "shortcut learning" behaviors in high-stakes domains like healthcare and developing causal interventions to mitigate them. I aim to move beyond aggregate performance metrics (like accuracy) to ensure models are robust, interpretable, and clinically trustworthy.

Selected Publications & Projects
Medical Imaging & Explainable AI

Interpretability-Driven Audit of Pneumonia Detection

Developed a comprehensive auditing framework for pediatric pneumonia detection. Despite achieving a high AUROC (0.97), initial models relied on spurious radiographic text markers. Using GradCAM++ and RISE, coupled with causal marker injection experiments, I isolated these shortcuts. The final model was retrained on sanitized data, significantly improving specificity (0.49 → 0.86).

NLP & Mechanistic Interpretability

Causal Auditing of Latent Affect in LLMs

Investigated internal representations of "affect" within GPT-2. Identified an anger-related direction in the residual stream (Layer 8) and used activation steering to intervene during inference. The study demonstrates that steering this direction causally modulates discourse structure—such as sentence length and self-reference—rather than just changing categorical emotion labels.
